Executive coaching

Individualized coaching provides a one-on-one development opportunity tailored to the specific goals of the executive. Each session begins and ends with a clearly defined action plan to maximize time and results.

Process: An executive coaching process consists of scheduled meetings between the coach and the executive over an agreed-upon period of time. This process includes a number of consulting methods. Examples: needs assessment, definition of expectations, skill practices, videotaping, mentoring, modeling behaviors and 360 feedback.

Benefits:

  • Identify performance barriers to success
  • Increase awareness of verbal and nonverbal communication skills
  • Develop leadership skills
  • Create vision with ability to implement
  • Improve self-discipline and confidence
  • Hone people skills for growth and mentoring

Communication skills traninig

Having extensive knowledge of a subject matter does not equate with communicating it effectively. Your knowledge remains dormant, unless you feel comfortable, and confident with yourself, your material and your audience.

Target audience: Any individual who wants to increase their comfort level, manage anxiety, appear organized and exude confidence and credibility when presenting. A novice presenter or an experienced executive who presents often, will increase and strengthen their communication skills, guaranteed. Enroll up to 6 participants per consultant.

Topics:

  • Manage anxiety (Physical and Emotional)
  • Present with polish (Do you exude comfort, confidence and credibility?)
  • Know your audience (Do they know you?)
  • Choose the right words (Reduce fillers, i.e. um, uh. Increase tentative language awareness)
  • Mind map your message (Meet your brain, be concise)
  • Organize your thoughts (Where do you begin & how do you conclude?)
  • Use humor effectively and cautiously (Are you comfortable telling a joke, should you?)
  • Powerful non verbal body language (It’s not what you say, it’s how you say it)
  • Handling difficult questions and objections (Is there a heckler out there?)
  • Are you listening? (Do you remember what your colleagues and manager told you?)
  • Proper use of a podium and microphone (Can you move away from a podium?)
  • Increase your comfort level on a panel (Can you perform without a script?)
  • Can technical, scientific & financial presentations be interesting (Get & keep their attention)

Executive presence training

To have Professional Presence, one must exhibit confidence, competence, and credibility under pressure, as well as under normal business conditions. You must communicate in an effective verbal, and non-verbal manner. Non-verbal body language has the most impact on our communication.

When making a first impression, critical perception occurs in 30 seconds. Our physical appearance, including attire, posture, eye contact and handshake, are the first non-verbal characteristics observed by others. Too much technology can and has depersonalized many of our relationships. Do you know how your clients perceive you and your organization? How about your peers, colleagues and manager? What could you do differently to enhance your presence in and out of the office?

Topics:

  • Make first impressions lasting impressions
  • Develop the art of self promotion
  • Define your image and develop the art of self promotion.
  • Using space, territory, mirroring and matching to make a connection
  • Language of presence, posture, handshakes and eye contact
  • Electronic Etiquette – The use and abuse of e-mail
  • Phone Presence: office, cell, conference and voice mail
  • Manage Dangling Conversations: gossip, grapevine and jokes
  • Damage Control, managing critical circumstances and sticky situations
  • Business travel etiquette
  • Business manners while dining
