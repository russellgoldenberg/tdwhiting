Professional Presence

Good posture not only take off 10 years & 10 pounds, it creates an instant impression of competence. - Susan Bixler and Lisa Scherrer Dugan

What is Professional Presence?

To have Professional Presence, one must exhibit confidence, competence, and credibility under pressure, as well as under normal business conditions. You must communicate in an effective verbal, and non-verbal manner. Non-verbal body language has the most impact on our communication.

When making a first impression, critical perception occurs in 30 seconds. Our physical appearance, including attire, posture, eye contact and handshake, are the first non-verbal characteristics observed by others.

Too much technology can and has depersonalized many of our relationships.

Do you know how your clients perceive you and your organization? How about your peers, colleagues and manager? What could you do differently to enhance your presence in and out of the office?

Suggested Topics to be covered:

This half day program can be designed to extend to a full day. The selected topics will dictate the length of the program.

Make first impressions lasting impressions
 Develop the art of self promotion
 Define your image and develop the art of self promotion.
 Using space, territory, mirroring and matching to make a connection
 Language of presence, posture, handshakes and eye contact
 Electronic Etiquette – The use and abuse of e-mail
 Phone Presence: office, cell, conference and voice mail
 Manage Dangling Conversations: gossip, grapevine and jokes
 Damage Control, managing critical circumstances and sticky situations
 Business travel etiquette
 Business manners while dining
